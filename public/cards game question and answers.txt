Q1) 2 of hearts).  Body and soul.
       The Vedic literatures explain that the body - soul combinations may be
       compared to a chariot.
       What are the comparisons.?
Ans). Body   ————- chariot
         Horses ————— senses
         Reins ————mind
         Driver ————— intelligence
         Passenger ————- soul.

Q2) 3 of hearts). 3 miseries.
         What are the 3 kinds of miseries that we, the-humans are subjected to in 
          this   material world.? Give with explanations.
Ans). 1). Adhiatmic = miseries caused by our own body and mind.
          2). Adhibhautic = miseries caused by other living entities.
          3). Adhidaivic. =. miseries caused by natural disturbances.

Q5) 6 of hearts). 6 Opulences.

       What does Bhagavan mean?
       How did Parasara Muni explain this?

Ans).  Parasara Muni explained that Bhagavan is one who is full in 6 opulences:
           Full strength
           Full  fame
           Full wealth
           Full knowledge
           Full beauty
           Full renunciation.
           Krishna, while on Earth displayed all 6.


Q6). 7 of hearts).  Sloka number 7.
    
         What sloka is this? Please give the translation.
       
         karpanya - dosopahata - svabhavah 
           prcchami tvam dharma - sammudha - cetah 
         yac chreyah syan niscitam bruhi tan me 
            sisyas te ‘ ham sadhi mam tvam prapannam 
      
 Ans).   Sloka number 2.7
   
             Now I am confused about my duty and have lost all composure because 
             of miserly weakness. In this condition I am asking You to tell me for
             certain what is best for me. Now I am Your disciple, and a soul 
             surrendered unto You. Please instruct me.

Q7).  8 of hearts).  8 elements
       
           What are the combined gross and subtle elements of our material body?
 
Ans).  The 5 gross elements are earth, water, fire, air and ether.
           The 3 subtle elements are mind, intelligence and false ego.
           8 in all.

Q11) Queen of hearts).  Queen of fasting.

          What is the Queen of all  fastings? Explain in 2, 3 sentences.

Ans).   Ekadashi.
         1). It is the 11 th day of the lunar fortnight and thus occurs twice a month.
         2). It is the day of ‘ Lord Hari’, when Lord Krishna is especially pleased to
               offer spiritual benedictions to those who perform austerities and
               devotional activities for His satisfaction.
         3). Devotees fast from grains and maximise their hearing and chanting and
               do other seva.
